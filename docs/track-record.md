Sample size is 10, on a single theme, with no control group — so read this as *promising, not proven*. That's the point of publishing it.

## Honesty notes

1. **Backtrack detection is coincident, not anticipatory.** The predictive content lives in the *setup* score (the 78 before the ceasefire); the flag documents completion. Lifecycle tracking was added in June precisely because scores lingered after completed backtracks.
2. **Cadence is spot, not continuous.** The 3-week gap (Jul 1 → Jul 20) came from expired automation credentials; the Jul 20 reading is marked `manual-research-run` in the data itself.
3. **One test is still open at publication time**: the Jul 20 reading assigned ~45% to a new tariff-front trigger around the Section 122 authority expiry (Jul 24, 2026).
4. **What would make this rigorous** (roadmap): labeled outcomes at fixed horizons (24/48/72h) for every reading, a calibration curve once n grows, and a control theme the instrument should stay silent on.
